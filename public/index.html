About

Who we are

This portal, created under the guidelines of the Punjab Environmental Protection Agency, serves as a one-stop solution for the registration and licensing of stakeholders involved in the production, consumption, recycling, and collection of single-use plastics. It ensures compliance with the Punjab Environmental Protection Act, 1997.

  • Ban on specific single-use plastics like disposable cups, candy sticks, and more.
  • Online registration for Producers, Consumers, Recyclers, and Collectors.
  • Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) to ensure sustainable plastic management.
  • Guidelines and incentives to encourage eco-friendly practices.

Plastic pollution has become one of the most pressing environmental issues. By adopting these regulations, Punjab aims to:

  • Reduce non-recyclable plastic waste.
  • Promote sustainable production and consumption cycles.
  • Protect our ecosystems from hazardous pollution.

Plastic License Application Process

Follow these simple steps to complete your application.

STEP 1

Login/Sign Up

Begin your application by creating an account or logging into our platform. Enjoy a seamless experience with user-friendly navigation.

STEP 2

Applicant Detail

Provide your personal details, including contact information and other required data to move forward in the process.

STEP 3

Business Detail

Enter your business details, such as entity type, address, and registration requirements. Tailored for producers, recyclers, and others.

Documents Upload

Upload all required documents, such as business registration, identification, and other relevant certifications.

Payment Detail

Enter your payment details and complete the transaction to finalize your application process.

Review all entered details and submit your application. Our team will review and get back to you with updates.

Frequently Asked Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

All stakeholders involved in the production, consumption, collection, and recycling of single-use plastic products must register with the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Punjab. This includes producers, consumers (commercial/bulk users), collectors, and recyclers.

Producers must:
  • Register with the EPA Punjab within one month of starting operations.
  • Implement an Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) plan for plastic waste collection and recycling.
  • Ensure at least 20% recycled material in their products.
  • Conduct public awareness campaigns about proper disposal of plastics.

Yes, the regulations ban several single-use plastic items, including:
  • Plastic flags
  • Disposable cups and plates
  • Plastic cutlery (e.g., forks, spoons, straws)
  • Multi-layered packaging
  • Thin plastic bags (less than 75 microns)

Recyclers must:
  • Register with the EPA Punjab.
  • Ensure proper segregation of risk and non-risk plastic waste.
  • Use safe and environmentally sound methods for recycling, especially for food-contact plastics.
  • Avoid recycling hazardous plastic waste, such as medical or chemical containers.

Consumers, especially commercial users, must:
  • Register with the EPA Punjab if consuming bulk quantities of single-use plastics.
  • Segregate plastic waste at the source.
  • Dispose of recyclable waste through registered recyclers.
  • Use alternatives to banned single-use plastic products.

Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) is a framework that holds producers accountable for the collection, recycling, and proper disposal of plastic waste generated from their products. Producers must submit their EPR plans to the EPA Punjab and ensure compliance.
